Alzheimer's Awareness Run and Walk

The Alzheimer's Association is a charity that is bringing awareness to the degenerative mind disease called Alzheimers. Alzheimers is a disease that affects the brain, causing the afflicted to lose memory capacity, some thinking ability, and causes a change in their behavior. The most common form of Alzheimers is dementia, however, Alzheimer's Association stresses that this is not a common thing that happens along with aging. Some symptons to look out for of Alzheimer patients are memory loss that disrupt daily life, challenges in planning or solving problems, confusion with time or place, and etc.

The Alzheimer's Awareness Run and Walk is a run sponsered by Alzheimers Association in hopes to raise awareness for the mind disease Alzheimers. The event will be a 5 kilometer course that is meant to encourage all people, young and old, to participate. The event will have multiple stations set along the way with water for the participants who either decide to run or walk the event. The event is designed so participants can take breaks along the course if needed.

The event will be held on June 2, 2018 at 11 a.m. in Downtown Greenville. The event should span around 2 to 3 hours due to the distribution of shirts to participants after the event is completed the event will span as long as participants need it to.
